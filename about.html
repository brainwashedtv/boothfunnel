About

We built BoothFunnel because venue marketing is broken.

Bars and restaurants spend thousands a month on ads that play to strangers. Meanwhile their actual customers — the ones who chose to be there on a Friday night — are taking selfies that mention nobody. We thought that was a strange thing to leave on the table.

The story

A bar in Brooklyn, a printer in a closet.

[Founder bio placeholder — replace with the real origin story before launch.]

The first version of BoothFunnel was a hacked-together iPad and a thermal printer in a Brooklyn cocktail bar. Customers loved it. The owner kept asking us to fix bugs in it. Eventually we asked: what if this were a real product, and any bar in the country could plug one in?

Three years later, that's what BoothFunnel is. Hundreds of venues, millions of branded photos, one core idea: the cheapest customer acquisition channel a venue has is the one already standing at the bar.

What we believe

Three things we won't compromise on.

Hardware that just works

If your booth fails on a Saturday, that's our emergency, not yours. We over-engineer for floors that don't tolerate downtime.

Privacy by default

Customers consent before sharing. Photos delete on a rolling 90 days. We're a data processor, not a data hoarder.

Pricing you can predict

Flat monthly fee, no per-print surprises, cancel any time. We win when you keep us. Simple.
