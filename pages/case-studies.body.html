Bar · Brooklyn, NY

The Lantern Room turned the Saturday queue into a marketing channel.

Marcus Webb opened The Lantern Room in 2023 and spent the first year buying Instagram ads. He swapped them for a booth in February.

What changed in 60 days: 4,200 branded photos printed, 1,900 shared to Instagram, hashtag use up 8× from his pre-booth baseline. He cancelled $1,400/month in Instagram ad spend and kept the booth.

"We pay for the booth in two Saturday nights. The rest of the month is just free Instagram impressions for the bar."
Marcus Webb · Owner, The Lantern Room
Restaurant group · Austin, TX

Quincey's group rolled out 5 booths across their concepts.

A 5-restaurant group that wanted one booth per location, each with its own brand inside the same operator dashboard.

What changed in 90 days: 18,000 prints across the group, hashtag mentions across all five brands tripled, and they discovered which two locations actually drove the most social engagement (and which two needed a layout change to put the booth somewhere customers could find it).

"The data alone was worth it. We learned more about our customers in 90 days than in the previous three years."
Sasha Quincey · Director of Brand, Quincey Restaurant Group
Event venue · Chicago, IL

Halsted Hall raised average wedding upsell by $1,800.

A historic venue doing 80+ weddings a year. They added the booth to every package as a "social media memory" perk.

What changed in 6 months: 92% of bookings opted into the upsell, $1,800 average lift per event, and 70+ couples came back the next month for the photo gallery — surfacing a leakage point in their post-event email flow they hadn't known about.

"We charge $250 to add the booth to a package. Our cost is roughly $40 of prints. The math is the easiest part of running this venue."
Lila Park · GM, Halsted Hall
