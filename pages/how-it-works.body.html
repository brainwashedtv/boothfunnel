What's in the box

One unit, everything you need.

The booth ships as a single freestanding unit. Inside the carton: the booth, a power cable, a Wi-Fi-or-cellular router (you pick), one starter pack of branded print media, a quick-start card, and a return label in case you ever cancel.

No mounting, no drilling, no electrician. The unit lives wherever there's a 3-foot square and a power outlet. We've seen them at the end of bars, in the corner of dining rooms, by the entrance, by the photo wall, in the queue. They're heavy enough to stay put and light enough that two staff can move them between rooms.

A typical Saturday

What it looks like on the floor.

A guest walks up. The booth shows your venue's name, your colors, a quick "tap to start". They take three shots, pick a print frame, and choose to text it to themselves or skip the share — their call. The branded print drops out in 8 seconds. They walk back to the bar.

If they shared: the digital version lands as a watermarked image on their phone, ready to post. If they didn't: the physical print still has your brand on it. Either way, your night just turned into a small piece of someone else's social feed.

In your dashboard the next morning: prints made, shares sent, hashtag mentions, estimated reach. Per night, per server's section if you tag it, per promo if you swap frames mid-shift.

Common questions

Operator FAQs.

What if it breaks on a Friday night?

Call the support line, we ship a replacement on the next morning's flight if it's a hardware fault. We've never had a venue lose more than one shift.

Do I need to train staff?

No. The booth is fully customer-operated. Your staff's only job is keeping a stack of print paper topped up — like a receipt printer.

Can I take it to off-site events?

Yes. The unit is portable and the cellular router means it works without venue Wi-Fi. Catering teams use this constantly.

How fast is the print?

~8 seconds from the last shot to a finished print in hand. The booth holds a queue of 4 prints so a busy night doesn't back up.
